ecoDebt Score Calculation

The ecoDebt score (eD) is a measure of envrionmental debt for each dorm. The lower the eD score, the less impact that dorm has on the environment. The eD score is calculated using a comprehensive approach to ensure fairness and accuracy:

  1. For any given dorm, we first collect utilities usage data. This information includes heating, cooling, natural gas, and general electricity use.

  2. Next, we compute how many Metric Tons of CO2 are emitted for each type of utility usage.

  3. Normalize the data by finding the z-score to ensure all eD scores are relative.

  4. Multiply the z-score for each utility by its corresponding proportion to the total Metric Tons of CO2. Sum these products for all utilities to create a preliminary ecoDebt score.

  5. Apply the logarithmic function to the preliminary score. This mathematical function moderates the scale of progress, making initial environmental improvements more impactful than later ones. This ensures that advancing from a small to a medium eD score is easier than progressing from a medium to a high score. The logarithmic function creates a more balanced and fair scoring system that encourages continuous improvement.
